# What to do when a top-up fails at payment

If a top-up is declined or fails before the credit is sent, no money leaves your account — the charge is only authorised once the top-up is confirmed delivered.

**Common reasons a top-up fails:**
- Your card issuer declined the payment. International or first-time purchases are sometimes blocked for security; approving the transaction in your bank app and retrying usually works.
- The recipient number or operator was entered incorrectly, so no matching product was found.
- The chosen amount is outside the range the operator allows.
- The operator's top-up service was temporarily unavailable.

**What to do:**
1. Check that the number, country code, and operator are correct.
2. Try a different payment method from **Account → Payment methods**.
3. Wait a few minutes and retry — temporary operator outages usually clear quickly.

**Pending charges:** If you see a pending authorisation on your card for a failed top-up, it is released automatically, typically within 1–5 business days depending on your bank. It is not an actual charge.

**Still failing?** If every attempt fails with a valid card and correct number, contact support with the recipient number and approximate time so we can check the operator status for that route.
